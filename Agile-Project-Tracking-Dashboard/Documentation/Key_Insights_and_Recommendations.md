# Key Insights and Business Recommendations

## Key Insights

- Sprint completion percentage provides a fast view of whether committed backlog items are being delivered.
- Story point completion gives a stronger delivery signal than task count alone because it accounts for work complexity.
- High-risk and overdue tasks should be reviewed together because risky tasks are more likely to affect project delivery timelines.
- Testing and In Progress queues can highlight workflow bottlenecks before sprint closure.
- Estimated versus actual hours helps evaluate planning accuracy and team utilization.
- Delay reason tracking supports continuous improvement by showing whether blockers come from dependencies, scope clarification, requirement changes, or resource constraints.
- Team productivity score helps compare completion, story point delivery, and utilization in a single KPI.

## Business Recommendations

- Review sprint completion rate and story point completion during sprint reviews.
- Use backlog tracking to reprioritize high-risk overdue tasks before they affect stakeholder commitments.
- Discuss recurring delay reasons during retrospectives and assign process improvement actions.
- Track resource utilization weekly to reduce overload and rebalance sprint assignments.
- Use bottleneck analysis to reduce excessive work in progress and improve flow efficiency.
- Monitor average cycle time to identify whether delivery speed is improving across sprints.
- Publish the dashboard to Power BI Service for recurring stakeholder reporting and KPI monitoring.

## Suggested Interview Explanation

This project demonstrates how Agile delivery data can be transformed into an operational dashboard for sprint tracking, backlog management, risk monitoring, and stakeholder reporting. It combines Excel-based data preparation, Power BI dashboard design, and DAX measures to support Scrum and project coordination decisions.
